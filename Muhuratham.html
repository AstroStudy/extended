The Tara System in Vedic Astrology

The Tara (star) system in Vedic astrology determines the relationship between two stars based on their positions in the 27 Nakshatras (lunar mansions). This system is used to find auspicious timings for various life events.

Janma Tara

Indicates birth, health, and vitality. Can be challenging but also transformative.

Sampat Tara

Represents wealth, prosperity, and material gains. Generally favorable.

Vipat Tara

Signifies obstacles, dangers, and challenges. Requires caution.

Kshema Tara

Indicates well-being, prosperity, and success in ventures.

Pratyak Tara

Suggests opposition, obstacles, and need for perseverance.

Sadhana Tara

Represents accomplishment through effort and spiritual practices.

Naidhana Tara

Associated with loss, destruction, and endings. Requires careful handling.

Mitra Tara

Indicates friendship, support, and beneficial connections.

Ati Mitra Tara

Represents deep friendship, great support, and highly favorable conditions.

Note: This is a simplified representation for educational purposes. For accurate astrological consultations, please consult a professional Vedic astrologer.
